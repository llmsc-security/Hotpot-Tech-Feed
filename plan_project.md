# Hotpot Tech Feed — Project Overview

_A friendly daily digest of computer science news, blogs, papers, and lab announcements — without the noise._

## What is Hotpot Tech Feed?

Hotpot Tech Feed is a website + daily email service that helps you keep up with computer science. It watches the places where new ideas appear — top university labs, industry research groups, engineering blogs, conferences, and tech news — and sends you a short, personalized summary every morning.

You don't have to follow ten Twitter accounts, refresh arXiv, or hop between blogs. We do the watching. You get the highlights.

## Who is it for?

Anyone working in or curious about computer science:

- **Researchers and PhD students** who want to keep up with their subfield without drowning in arXiv.
- **Software engineers and ML practitioners** who want the best engineering blogs and applied papers in one place.
- **Students and learners** who want a curated window into what's important right now.
- **Tech leads and product folks** who want trends and announcements without reading every paper.

You tell us what kind of reader you are — and what you care about — and we shape the feed around that.

## What problem does it solve?

Computer science moves too fast for any one person to follow. The good stuff is scattered across:

- arXiv, OpenReview, conference proceedings
- University lab blogs (Berkeley BAIR, Stanford NLP, CMU, MIT CSAIL, Tsinghua, ...)
- Industry labs (DeepMind, OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta FAIR, ByteDance Seed, ...)
- Engineering blogs (Cloudflare, Stripe, Netflix, Meta Engineering, ...)
- News sites, Hacker News, Reddit, WeChat, Xiaohongshu

Most people give up and rely on whatever Twitter happens to surface. That's noisy and biased. Hotpot replaces that with a calm, trusted, daily summary you actually have time to read.

## What can you do with it?

- **Subscribe by topic** — pick the areas you care about (machine learning, systems, theory, security, HCI, and so on) and we narrow the feed to those.
- **Subscribe by content type** — papers only, blogs only, or a mix.
- **Follow specific labs** — always include announcements from BAIR, Stanford NLP, DeepMind, or whichever labs you trust.
- **Get a daily email** — a short list of titles, links, and one-or-two-sentence AI summaries, delivered each morning.
- **Read AI-generated commentary** — every item gets a short note explaining what it is, why it matters, and how it relates to recent work.
- **Ask follow-up questions** — every item has a chat button so you can ask the assistant for more context.
- **Vote and refine** — thumbs-up, thumbs-down, or "less from this source" to tune your feed over time.
- **Contribute new sources** — found a great blog or lab we don't track? Submit it. If the community finds it useful, it joins the rotation.

## How it works (in plain language)

Three things happen quietly in the background:

1. **Watch.** We connect to hundreds of sources and check them on a schedule — RSS feeds, arXiv, lab websites, GitHub releases, and select Chinese-language platforms.
2. **Tidy up.** New items are de-duplicated (the same paper gets covered ten times in one week — we collapse those), tagged by topic, and given a short summary by an AI assistant.
3. **Deliver.** Every morning, we build a personalized digest for you based on your subscriptions and how you've voted in the past, and email it.

You can also browse the full feed on the website any time and use filters to dig into specific topics or labs.

## Languages

English is the primary language. We also pull from selected Chinese-language sources (WeChat public accounts, Xiaohongshu, and Chinese tech publications like 机器之心 and 量子位) so bilingual users don't miss what's happening in the Chinese CS community.

## How you can contribute

Hotpot is meant to be community-shaped. There are three ways to help:

- **Submit a source** — a lab, a blog, a publication you wish we tracked. We review, then put it on a one-week probation visible to opt-in users before promoting it to the main feed.
- **Vote on items** — your votes shape ranking and improve other readers' feeds in the same topics.
- **Flag low-quality content** — items flagged enough times go to a moderation queue, and persistent bad sources get downranked or paused.

Your contribution history builds reputation. Trusted contributors see their submissions promoted faster.

## What's coming

The project is rolling out in stages so we get the basics right before adding everything:

1. **Stage 1** — A working English-language feed: arXiv + a starter set of blogs and labs, with a daily email digest.
2. **Stage 2** — Accounts, personal subscriptions, persona presets (Researcher / Engineer / Learner / Generalist), and the star-lab list.
3. **Stage 3** — Open contributions, moderation, and reputation.
4. **Stage 4** — Chinese-language sources (WeChat, Xiaohongshu, and Chinese publications) and a bilingual interface.
5. **Stage 5** — Polishing: better dedup, smarter recommendations, source health dashboard, AI commentary turned on for every item.

## Privacy

We keep things minimal:

- Your email address is used to send the digest and verify your account. Nothing else.
- Your votes and reading history shape your feed only — we don't sell or share them.
- You can export everything you've contributed and delete your account at any time.
